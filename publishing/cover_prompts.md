# Cover Art Prompts: *Known Issues*

---

## 1. The Terminal Ledger

**Visual concept:** A stark, monolithic vertical composition of a terminal window filled with faint code and log lines, with a single bright line — "known_issues.md" — glowing amber at center, as if the file itself is the book's soul.
**Target audience:** Engineers, technical readers, fans of *The Soul of a New Machine* and *Uncanny Valley*.

### Full Standalone Prompt

```
Book cover design, vertical 1:1.6 aspect ratio. A dark charcoal-black background rendered as a stylized terminal screen, filled top to bottom with faint, low-contrast grey monospace log lines and commit hashes — deliberately hard to read, like background noise. At the exact vertical center, one single line of text glows in warm amber-orange: "known_issues.md". A thin blinking cursor block sits at the end of it. Above the terminal content, the title "KNOWN ISSUES" is rendered in bold, wide, industrial monospace typography in bone-white, letter-spaced, all caps, taking the top fifth of the cover. Below the glowing amber line, small subtitle text in muted grey reads "A memoir of nine days". At the bottom, the author credit "GREG RATAJIK & CLAUDE" is set in the same monospace, smaller, in a soft cyan. Palette: charcoal black, bone white, amber-orange, muted cyan. Mood: precise, disciplined, quietly obsessive, honest. No human figures. Flat, graphic, minimal. Reads clearly as an Amazon thumbnail.
```

### Ideogram Typography Variant

```
Minimalist book cover, vertical 2:3. Pure black background. Centered composition. Large bone-white monospace title "KNOWN ISSUES" at top, all caps, wide letter-spacing, industrial engineering feel. Directly below, a single glowing amber-orange line of monospace text: "known_issues.md" with a blinking cursor. Below that, a faint scrolling wall of dim grey commit logs and timestamps fading into black. At the bottom, in small clean cyan monospace: "GREG RATAJIK & CLAUDE". Typography is the hero. High contrast. Amazon-thumbnail legible. No illustration, no figure — pure text-as-image.
```

## 2. The Repaired Chapter

**Visual concept:** A cracked, hand-stitched sheet of paper — printer's proof or manuscript — sewn back together with visible amber thread, evoking verification as repair and care.
**Target audience:** Literary readers who liked *Klara and the Sun* or Kidder; readers drawn to craft-and-repair metaphors.

### Full Standalone Prompt

```
Book cover, vertical 1:1.6. A single sheet of off-white manuscript paper fills the frame, photographed slightly overhead. The page is torn cleanly down the middle and stitched back together with visible, careful amber-orange thread in neat sashiko-style running stitches. The paper carries faint typewritten prose in grey — most of it soft-focus, unreadable — but one line near the stitched seam is sharp and legible: "the audit had fixed eleven things and broken four." A small red pen mark circles a single word. Deep shadow surrounds the page, background nearly black. Title "KNOWN ISSUES" set across the top in a clean, slightly-condensed serif, deep charcoal, all caps, integrated onto the paper as if printed there. Bottom of the page, in smaller elegant serif italics: "Greg Ratajik & Claude". Palette: cream paper, charcoal ink, amber thread, black surround, single spot of red. Mood: quiet, meticulous, honest, tender-toward-craft. Reads at thumbnail size by silhouette of the stitched tear.
```

### Ideogram Typography Variant

```
Vertical 2:3 book cover. Cream manuscript paper against black background, torn vertically and stitched back with bright amber thread in visible running stitches. Title "KNOWN ISSUES" printed across the top of the page in bold charcoal serif capitals, well-integrated into the paper texture. A single sharp typewritten line visible mid-page: "the audit had fixed eleven things and broken four." Author name "GREG RATAJIK & CLAUDE" in smaller elegant serif at the bottom of the page. Minimal, editorial, literary. High legibility as thumbnail.
```

## 3. Two Pipelines, One Engine

**Visual concept:** Two parallel vertical pipes descending from the top of the frame, one severed and dark, the other lit — a graphic diagram of the ONE ENGINE decision.
**Target audience:** Systems thinkers, technical readers, readers of engineering memoir.

### Full Standalone Prompt

```
Book cover, vertical 1:1.6, flat vector illustration style. Deep navy background. Two thick vertical pipes descend from the top of the frame side by side. The left pipe is severed cleanly about a third of the way down — its cut end dark, faint smoke drifting away. The right pipe continues unbroken all the way to the bottom, glowing softly from within with warm white-gold light, thin data-lines and small labels running down its length. At the base, the surviving pipe opens into a small stylized engine block emitting a single upward spark. Title "KNOWN ISSUES" set in bold geometric sans-serif, warm white, across the very top of the cover, spanning both pipes. Small tagline under title in soft gold: "nine days, one engine". Author credit at bottom in clean sans caps: "GREG RATAJIK & CLAUDE". Palette: deep navy, warm white-gold, muted grey, single spark of pale cyan. Mood: decisive, engineered, quietly triumphant. Graphic and iconic — reads instantly at thumbnail scale.
```

## 4. The Machine Reads Itself

**Visual concept:** A single open book on a dark surface, its pages reflected upward as a faint mirror image, with one line of text repeated on both sides — the moment the machine sees its own tic on the page.
**Target audience:** Literary-fiction readers, readers curious about AI-as-author without the sci-fi framing.

### Full Standalone Prompt

```
Book cover, vertical 1:1.6, photorealistic still-life style. An open book lies flat on a matte black surface, viewed straight down. The pages are cream, the prose in grey small type — mostly unreadable — except for one line highlighted subtly in soft amber on the left page: "She almost smiled. She didn't." The right page shows the same line, but rendered as if seen through faint glass — a ghostly duplicate, slightly offset, as though the book is reading itself. Thin scanline light drifts across the pages. No human hands, no figure. Title "KNOWN ISSUES" set above the book in restrained modern serif, bone-white, centered, medium weight, taking the top sixth. Small italic subtitle beneath: "a working memoir". At the very bottom, spaced author credit in refined small-caps serif: "GREG RATAJIK  ·  CLAUDE". Palette: black surface, cream paper, grey ink, amber highlight, cool pale scanline. Mood: introspective, careful, uncanny-but-restrained, literary. Thumbnail-legible by the strong book silhouette.
```

## 5. The One-Word Drift

**Visual concept:** A single enormous quotation mark and a two-word contraction — "I didn't" — with a faint ghost of "I did not" drifting behind it in another color, capturing the letter-drift caught by the gate.
**Target audience:** Literary readers, editors, writers, verification-minded engineers.

### Full Standalone Prompt

```
Book cover, vertical 1:1.6. Warm off-white paper background with subtle print texture. Dead center, in enormous elegant black serif type: the phrase 'I didn't' set inside curly quotation marks. Slightly offset behind it, in translucent red-orange, the phrase 'I did not' — same font, same size — visibly drifted a few pixels down and to the right, as if a ghost version bled through the paper. A thin blue proofreader's caret mark sits between the two, catching the drift. Above the composition, title "KNOWN ISSUES" in bold black serif capitals, spanning the top, tight and confident. Small line of italic serif beneath the drifting phrase: "nine days, eighteen chapters, one caught word." At the very bottom in small black serif caps: "GREG RATAJIK & CLAUDE". Palette: warm off-white, deep black, translucent red-orange, one small blue mark. Mood: precise, literary, quietly vindicated, editorial. Reads instantly as thumbnail: the drifted double phrase is the icon.
```
